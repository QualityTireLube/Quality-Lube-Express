Louisiana State Inspection Requirements

Failed a Louisiana State Inspection?

We will explain what failed, why it failed, and what is required to pass — no guessing.

Click a topic below to see what passes and what fails. Each section expands when selected.

What You Must Bring

To perform a Louisiana state inspection, the driver must present:

  • Valid driver's license
  • Current vehicle registration
  • Current valid insurance

If any of these items are missing, the inspection cannot be performed.

Warning Lights

PASS:

  • No emissions or safety-related warning lights

FAIL:

  • Check Engine light ON

Note: Clearing codes right before inspection may cause a failure due to unreadiness.

Readiness Tests (Emissions Computer Checks)

PASS:

  • No Check Engine light
  • Required readiness monitors show "Ready"
  • No active emissions fault codes

FAIL:

  • One or more required monitors show "Not Ready"
  • Check Engine light is ON
  • Active emissions-related trouble codes present
  • No communication to the engine computer

Clearing codes does not fix the problem — it resets the tests.

What Is a Readiness Test?

A readiness test is a self-check run by your vehicle's computer to verify emissions systems are working correctly. These tests run automatically while you drive. The state inspection checks whether these tests have run and passed.

Readiness Monitors – What They Are & What They Do

Gasoline vehicles run multiple readiness monitors. Each monitor checks a specific emissions or engine system while you drive.

  • Misfire Monitor: Detects engine misfires that increase emissions.
  • Fuel System Monitor: Checks proper fuel delivery.
  • Comprehensive Components Monitor: Checks sensors and electrical components.
  • Catalyst Monitor: Verifies catalytic converter efficiency.
  • Heated Catalyst Monitor (if equipped): Checks heated catalytic converters.
  • EVAP Monitor: Detects fuel vapor leaks.
  • Secondary Air Monitor (if equipped): Checks cold-start air injection system.
  • Oxygen Sensor Monitor: Verifies oxygen sensor operation.
  • Oxygen Sensor Heater Monitor: Checks oxygen sensor heaters.
  • EGR / VVT Monitor: Verifies exhaust gas recirculation or valve timing systems.

Note: Not all vehicles have every monitor.

Why Readiness Tests Are NOT Ready (Most Common Reasons)
  • Repairs were done recently
  • Check Engine light was cleared
  • Battery was replaced or disconnected
  • Vehicle was jump-started
  • Weak or failing battery
  • Sluggish or slow engine starts
  • Short trips only (no highway driving)
How to Make Readiness Tests Run and Pass

While every vehicle is different, most require:

  • Cold start
  • City driving
  • Highway driving
  • Steady cruising speeds
  • Normal acceleration and deceleration

Note: This usually takes 50–100 miles or several drive cycles.

Important to Know
  • Driving longer does not guarantee readiness if a problem still exists
  • Clearing codes right before inspection almost always causes a failure
  • Readiness is controlled by the vehicle computer, not the inspector
What We Can Do
  • Check readiness status before inspection
  • Identify which monitors are incomplete
  • Advise how to complete drive cycles
  • Diagnose issues preventing readiness
Bottom Line

If your vehicle fails for readiness:

  • Nothing is wrong with the inspection
  • The vehicle simply has not completed its self-tests yet
Windshield Damage (Most Common Failure)

FAIL if ANY of the following are present:

  • Any crack in the driver's direct line of sight
  • More than two chips, stars, or bullseyes larger than ½ inch
  • Any crack longer than 8 inches

Note: Cracks often spread due to heat, cold, and vibration.

Window Tint – Cars (Sedans, Coupes, Hatchbacks)

PASS:

  • Windshield: Clear strip only, maximum 5 inches from the top
  • Front side windows: 25% light transmission
  • Rear side windows: 25% light transmission
  • Rear glass: 12% light transmission
  • Reflectivity: Maximum 20%

FAIL:

  • Tint darker than allowed
  • Red or amber tint
  • Reflective or mirrored tint

Note: Tint is measured with a state-approved light meter.

Window Tint – Trucks & SUVs

PASS:

  • Windshield: Clear strip only, maximum 5 inches from the top
  • Front side windows: 25% light transmission
  • Rear side windows: Any darkness allowed
  • Rear glass: Any darkness allowed
  • Reflectivity: Maximum 20%

FAIL:

  • Windshield tint below the top strip
  • Red, amber, or mirrored tint
Brakes (Road Test Required)

The vehicle must be test driven during inspection.

PASS:

  • Vehicle stops straight when brakes are applied at 20 MPH

FAIL:

  • Pulling left or right
  • Grinding or abnormal noise
  • Weak braking performance
Tires

PASS:

  • Minimum 2/32" tread depth
  • Even wear
  • Tires do not extend beyond fenders

FAIL:

  • Bald tires
  • Exposed cords
  • Sidewall cracks or bulges
  • Tires extend beyond fenders
Exterior Lights

PASS:

  • All lights working
  • Correct color lenses

FAIL:

  • Burnt-out bulbs
  • Broken lenses
  • White light showing to the rear
  • Off-road or roof-mounted lights uncovered
Mirrors, Doors, and Seat Belts

PASS:

  • Mirrors secure and usable
  • Doors open, close, and latch properly
  • Seat belts present and functional

FAIL:

  • Missing mirrors
  • Doors that do not latch
  • Damaged or missing seat belts
Exhaust System

PASS:

  • Exhaust properly mounted
  • No excessive noise, smoke, or fumes

FAIL:

  • Leaks
  • Excessive noise
  • Smoke or exhaust entering the cabin
Emissions and Gas Cap

PASS:

  • Required emissions components present
  • Gas cap seals properly

FAIL:

  • Missing emissions equipment
  • Loose or leaking gas cap
What Is Outside the Inspection Station's Control
  • Existing vehicle damage
  • Tint installed beyond legal limits
  • Warning lights caused by vehicle faults
  • Readiness monitors not completed
  • Aftermarket modifications that violate state rules
